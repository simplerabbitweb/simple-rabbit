### Linkedin

Search behavior has shifted in a way that most service businesses haven't accounted for yet.

According to Capgemini's 2025 consumer trends report, 58% of consumers have replaced traditional search engines with AI tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews — when looking for products and services. For service businesses, where trust is the deciding factor before anyone picks up the phone, the implications are significant.

AI search doesn't work like Google. Instead of ranking ten websites and letting the user choose, it synthesizes information and surfaces one or two recommendations. Getting cited in that answer requires your website to be doing specific things: clear geographic and service-area signals, direct answers to the questions clients actually search for, consistent business information across your site and Google Business Profile, and content that demonstrates genuine expertise — not just keywords.

Vague positioning and generic service pages won't cut it. AI models need to be able to understand exactly who you serve and why you're credible before they'll surface your name.

The good news: most service business websites haven't optimized for this yet. The businesses that get ahead of it now will have a real advantage as AI search continues to grow.

If you're not sure whether your website is structured for AI visibility, that's a worthwhile thing to assess. The changes required often overlap with good web design practice anyway — clear positioning, specific copy, authoritative content.

When a prospect lands on a site that looks underbuilt, their brain quietly sets a number. Then you send your actual quote and it comes in above that number. That's not a pricing problem. That's a positioning problem.

Your website is doing the pre-sell. The question is whether it's doing it well.
